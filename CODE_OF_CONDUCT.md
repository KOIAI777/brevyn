# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

Contributors and maintainers are expected to keep project spaces constructive, specific, and respectful. Examples of acceptable behavior include:

- Giving actionable technical feedback and explaining tradeoffs.
- Respecting different experience levels, backgrounds, and viewpoints.
- Keeping issues and pull requests focused on the reported problem.
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and helping repair their impact.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language, deliberate disruption, publishing another person's private information, or using project spaces for unrelated promotion.

## Scope

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, reviews, and other spaces where someone represents Brevyn Community.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, issues, and contributions that do not follow this policy. Temporary or permanent participation restrictions may be applied when necessary.

For a private conduct report, use the repository's **Security > Report a vulnerability** channel and prefix the report title with `[Code of Conduct]`. This private channel is also used for conduct reports until a dedicated community contact is published. Maintainers will review reports confidentially and respond proportionately to the circumstances.
